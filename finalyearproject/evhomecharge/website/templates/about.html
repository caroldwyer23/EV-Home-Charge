Charging an electric vehicle (EV) at home in Ireland is relatively straightforward, but there are a few things you should know before getting started..

Once your charge point is installed, you can start renting your chargepoint to other ev users straight away....

  • Firstly, you will need a dedicated electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE), which is essentially a specialized charging station that is installed in your home. The EVSE will need to be connected to a power source, usually your home's electrical panel, and can be mounted on a wall or installed on a pedestal.
  • Secondly, you will need to choose the type of EVSE that suits your needs. There are several types of EVSEs available, ranging from basic models that simply provide a charging outlet to more advanced models that offer smart charging features and connectivity with your smartphone. Some popular brands of EVSEs in Ireland include EO Charging, Wallbox, and Zappi.
  • Once you have chosen an EVSE and had it installed, you will need to connect your electric vehicle to the EVSE using a charging cable. Most EVs come with a charging cable that is compatible with the standard Type 2 charging connector, which is used by most public charging stations in Ireland. If your EV doesn't come with a charging cable or you need an additional one, you can purchase one separately from an EV charging equipment supplier.
  • Finally, you will need to monitor your EV's charging progress to ensure that it is charging safely and efficiently. Many EVSEs come with built-in monitoring and reporting features, and some even allow you to schedule charging times or set charging limits to avoid overloading your home's electrical system.
